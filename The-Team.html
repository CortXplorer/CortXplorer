Our Lab

The current CortXplorer working group members

Prof. Max Happel

Group Leader

Max studied biology in Frankfurt am Main and neurobiology in Magdeburg. He received his PhD in systems sensory & behavioral physiology at the Leibniz Institute for Neurobiology (LIN) in Magdeburg in 2011 with prof. Frank Ohl. He was then a postdoc at the University of Oxford with Prof. Andrew King, funded by the German Academic Exchange Service (DAAD). Afterwards, he established his research group CortXplorer at LIN Magdeburg as a Leibniz Postdoc Fellow. The research of the team focuses on the functional neurophysiology of decision-making, learning and memory with an emphasis on cortical microcircuit processing. Besides doing science in the lab, Max collaborates with the Hessian Ministry of Education in the context of teacher training and school concept development and is active as a trainer in the field of digital learning, adult education and personal skills development for schools and vocational educational institutions with a focus on self-organized learning. 

Katrina Deane

PhD Candidate

Katrina Deane has been working on her PhD project since the end of 2018 on population dynamics in the auditory cortex during voltage gated calcium channel clustering and the subsequent shift of stochastic to deterministic firing behavior in pyramidal neurons. She completed her Master’s with us in 2018 on a project exploring differences between anesthetized and awake naive Mongolian gerbils’ auditory cortices in response to pure tones.
Katrina has been involved in several Long Night of Science outreach events in 2017, 2018, and 2019, and will help host a virtual Science Slam in the Long Night of Science 2021 as a function of her role as PhD Representative since 2020.

SABINA NOWAKOWSKA

Master's Student

Sabina studied BSc Biology and then MSc Bioinformatics and Theoretical Systems Biology at Imperial College London. Since 2019 she has been pursuing a masters degree in Integrative Neuroscience at OVGU. She works as HiWi at Laboratory for Sensory Physiology (OVGU) under prof. Kristine Krug’s supervision. She organises a monthly neuroscience seminar series IBIO Neuroassembly. She joined CortXplorers in the summer 2020 for a lab rotation and now she’s working on her master’s thesis with us.
